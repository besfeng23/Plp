# OTA Mapping Operator Playbook

## Creating a draft room mapping
Choose the channel and internal accommodation. Add at least one OTA room ID, room name, listing ID, or channel room code. Save as `draft` / `needs_review`.

## Reviewing a room mapping
Compare the OTA room name, ID, guest capacity, and internal accommodation. Move to `ready_for_review` / `in_review` when a staff member is actively checking it.

## Approving a room mapping
Approve only after the channel source and internal accommodation match. Approval is for future use only and does not sync inventory.

## Archiving a wrong mapping
Load the mapping, add a note explaining why it is wrong, and use Archive room mapping. Archived mappings should not be reused.

## Creating a draft rate mapping
Choose the channel and accommodation, enter the internal rate name, and add at least one OTA rate plan ID, rate plan name, or rate code.

## Reviewing a rate mapping
Check whether the rate plan belongs to the selected room/accommodation and whether currency, nights, refundable policy, and meal plan are accurate.

## Approving a rate mapping
Approve only after ambiguity is resolved. Approval does not change booking prices and does not publish rates.

## Handling missing OTA IDs
Save names or codes when available, note the missing ID, and keep the mapping in review until the channel source is confirmed.

## Handling mismatched room names
Do not approve. Add a note with the mismatch, keep the mapping in review or rejected status, and escalate to a manager.

## Handling rate plan ambiguity
Do not guess. Keep the mapping as draft or in review, record the ambiguous labels, and request channel documentation.

## Escalation steps
Escalate to the reservation lead when a mapping may affect guest-facing inventory, rate interpretation, or future OTA sync readiness. Never resolve uncertainty by using live OTA actions in Phase 2D.
